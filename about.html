The coach

Coach Daniel

A decade of proven player development — and a lifelong student of the game.

I bring a wealth of competitive experience to every session. From 2013 to 2023 I coached in Alexandria, directing team development across the U9–U16 age groups and coaching at every level of competitive youth soccer — including MLS NEXT, the top tier of the American youth game.

Alongside team coaching, I've spent the last 10 years on specialized individual training, mentoring players aged 7 to 21 from grassroots to elite levels. My focus never changes: refining technical skill, building tactical intelligence, and helping every athlete reach their personal goals.

Credentials

The license ladder

U.S. Soccer's coaching pathway runs from grassroots licenses through the professional A License. I've climbed it deliberately — E, D, then C — and I'm currently a candidate for the USSF B License, a credential held by a small fraction of youth coaches in the country.

  • USSF E — Held
  • USSF D — Held
  • USSF C — Held
  • USSF B — Candidate

Philosophy

Coach the player, not the drill

Ten years of individual training has taught me something club seasons can't: no two players need the same thing. A 9-year-old learning to love the ball, a 14-year-old fighting for a starting spot, and an 18-year-old preparing for college soccer are three different jobs.

What they share is my standard. Every session has a purpose the player can name. Every correction comes with a why. And I treat every athlete — grassroots or elite — like their goals matter, because they do.

  • Technical skill, refined
  • Tactical intelligence, built
  • Personal goals, reached
