On the pitch

Training that respects the game

No cone mazes for the highlight reel. I build every session backwards from what matches demand: clean technique, executed under pressure, with a decision attached.

1-on-1

Private training

The fastest way to change a player's trajectory. One coach, one player, and a program built around a real assessment — not a generic drill package.

The first session is an evaluation. I watch how you receive, strike, move, and decide, then map the two or three things that will unlock the most improvement. From there, every session has a stated objective and finishes with homework you can do on your own.

  • Individual development plan after the first session
  • Position-specific technical & tactical work
  • Progress check-ins parents actually understand
  • Training homework between sessions

2–4 players

Small group training

Some things can't be trained alone: pressure, competition, and the split-second choices that decide games. Small group sessions put players of a similar age and level together — small enough that nobody hides, big enough that every rep has an opponent.

I can place your player in a group, or build one from your own crew. Bring a teammate, a sibling, or two friends from your club — I match the level, and I never lower the standards.

  • Groups matched by age and level
  • 1v1, 2v2 and small-sided decision games
  • Constant individual correction within the group
  • Bring-your-own-group option

What a session looks like

  1. 00:00 Arrival & activation

    Ball rolling from minute one. Movement prep with a ball at the feet, never laps around a field.

  2. 00:10 Technical block

    The session's focus — first touch, striking, receiving on the half-turn — trained at high repetition with detail-level correction.

  3. 00:30 Decision block

    The same technique, now with a defender, a time limit, or a choice to make. This is where skill becomes game skill.

  4. 00:50 Game application

    Small-sided play with one constraint tied to the session objective. Players leave knowing exactly when to use what they trained.

  5. 01:00 Debrief & homework

    One thing done well, one thing to own before next session, and a homework exercise to bridge the gap.

The method

How I train

Every session is built around passing, receiving, and dribbling — the three techniques players execute most often in a match, regardless of position. Everything else is built on top of those.

Station 01

Three stations, one plan

Each session is divided into three stations, each tailored to the player's individual needs — so training stays focused and efficient, with no wasted reps.

Station 02

Tactics in every drill

A tactical component is woven into each drill, helping players understand the bigger picture behind every touch — not just what to do, but when and why.

Station 03

Scanning & decisions

Alongside the fundamentals, players train decision-making through scanning — reading their surroundings before the ball arrives, so they act faster and smarter under real match pressure.

Questions

Training FAQ

Where do sessions take place?

Sessions run at fields in and around Alexandria and Northern Virginia. I confirm exact locations when your schedule is set, and I keep travel reasonable for families.

How often should my player train?

Most players see strong results with one session per week alongside their club schedule. Players preparing for tryouts, a new level, or college often move to two. I'll give you an honest recommendation after the first evaluation — never a hard sell.

What does it cost?

Rates depend on format (private vs. small group) and session frequency. Send an inquiry and I'll include the current rate sheet with my reply — no games, no pressure.

Do you work with goalkeepers?

Field players are my specialty. Goalkeepers are welcome for distribution, footwork, and playing out of the back — for dedicated shot-stopping work, I'll point you to a specialist.

My player already trains with their club. Why add this?

Club training develops the team; it can't give one player hundreds of purposeful touches and individual correction in an hour. Private work fills exactly that gap — and I coordinate the focus with what your club is asking of your player.
